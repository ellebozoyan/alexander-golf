A golf hole has a beginning, a middle, and an end. You start at the TEE BOX. You aim for the GREEN. You hole out at the FLAG. Everything in between is the adventure.

The parts of a hole and what they mean

Tee box Where you START each hole. You put a small wooden TEE in the ground and balance the ball on top. First shot of the hole."This is where you start."
Fairway The short-mown grass where you WANT your ball to land. Easier to hit your next shot from here."Aim for the fairway."
Rough The taller grass on each side of the fairway. Harder to hit from. If your ball lands here = it's still in play, just trickier."Avoid if you can."
Bunker (sand trap) A sandy pit. Looks pretty but tricky to hit out of. Use a special wedge club. Rake the sand after you're done."Out of the sand. Rake it."
Water hazard A pond or stream. Ball lands here = you lose a stroke and have to drop another ball. AVOID."Stay dry."
Green The very short, smooth grass around the hole. You PUTT here (use the putter, roll the ball into the hole)."Roll, don't fly."
Hole & flag The tiny cup in the green you're trying to get the ball into. The flag (also called the "pin") helps you see it from far away."That's the target."
Cart path The paved path where golf carts drive. If your ball lands here = you get a free drop off the path."Off the path."

Golf grows with you

Golf has stages. Each one opens up more of the course. There is no rush. Every pro started on the practice green.

🟢

Putting Stage

What you learn here: the putter only. Rolling the ball into a hole from 3 feet. Then 6 feet. Then 10. Backyard putting green or kitchen carpet.

"Smooth roll. Just like rolling a marble."
⛳

Chipping Stage

What you learn here: short shots near the green. Ball flies a little, rolls a lot. Use a wedge. Backyard practice is perfect.

"Little fly. Big roll."
🏌️

Swing Stage

What you learn here: the full swing with irons. Hitting the ball further. Driving range practice. First holes on the course.

"Smooth full swing."
🏆

Course Stage

What you learn here: playing real holes. Reading the wind. Picking clubs. Keeping score. By now your swing, your patience, and your love of the game are all yours.

"My swing. My game. Forever."

Golf has clear rules — and even more important, it has ETIQUETTE (good manners). Once you know them, you can play with anyone, anywhere. People LOVE playing with a polite golfer.
🤫

1. Be QUIET when others swing

Don't talk, don't move, don't make noise. Stand STILL until the ball is hit. Then you can move. This is rule #1.

"Stand still. Stay quiet."
🗣️

2. Yell "FORE!" loud

If your shot heads toward ANYONE — yell "FORE!" as loud as you can. It warns them to duck. Always.

"FORE! Loud and fast."
🎯

3. Honors (who hits first)

At the tee box: the player who scored LOWEST on the last hole hits first. After the tee: the player FURTHEST from the hole hits next.

"Furthest plays first."
⏱️

4. Play ready, play fast

When it's your turn, be ready. Don't waste time. Walk briskly. A round shouldn't drag.

"Be ready."
🌱

5. Repair the green

If your ball makes a dent on the green when it lands, FIX IT. Use a small "ball mark repair" tool. Pop it up, then tap flat with the putter.

"Leave it better."
🕳️

6. Replace divots

When your club takes a chunk of grass out (a "divot") — PICK IT UP and place it back in the hole. Stomp it down.

"Put the grass back."
🏖️

7. Rake the bunker

After hitting out of sand, use the RAKE (it's right there) to smooth your footprints and the spot where your ball was. Leave it like you found it.

"Rake it smooth."
🚶

8. Don't walk on someone's line

On the green, don't step between another player's ball and the hole. Your footprints could change how their putt rolls.

"Walk around, not across."
🔢

9. Count every stroke

Every time you swing AT the ball (even if you miss) — that's a stroke. Count honestly. Tell your playing partner your score for each hole.

"Count them all."
🤝

10. Shake hands at the end

When the round is over, take off your hat, shake hands with everyone you played with, say "good game" — win, lose, or in between.

"Hat off. Hand shake. 'Good game.'"
Golf is the only sport where the rules ask you to call penalties on YOURSELF. That's why golfers respect each other so much. Honesty IS the game.
Every move in golf has a name and a feel. You'll learn the putt first (rolling the ball), then the chip (a small fly + roll), then the full swing (the big hit). Each one builds on the last.

The 6 essential moves

✋

The Grip

How you hold the club. Hands TOGETHER, pinkies and thumbs touching. Soft hands, not a death grip. Same grip for every club except the putter.

"Soft hands. Together."
🦵

The Stance

Feet shoulder-width apart. Knees slightly bent. Spine straight but TILTED forward from the hips. Ball in the middle of your feet.

"Soft knees. Tilt forward."
🟢

Putting

The putter ONLY. No wrists — your shoulders rock back and forth like a pendulum. Eyes over the ball. Smooth rhythm.

"Pendulum. No wrists."
⛳

Chipping

Like putting but with a wedge. Small backswing. Brush the grass under the ball. Ball flies a little, rolls a lot.

"Brush. Pop. Roll."
🏌️

The Full Swing

Back, up, and around — turn your shoulders. Then unwind through the ball. End with your belly facing the target. SMOOTH, not hard.

"Turn, unwind, finish."
🎯

Aim & Alignment

Before EVERY shot: pick a target. Line up your clubface at the target. Then line up your feet PARALLEL (like train tracks).

"Clubface. Then feet."

The Pre-Shot Routine (do it EVERY shot)

The 4 steps every golfer uses, every time:

  • 1. PICK — pick your target. Where do you want the ball to go?
  • 2. PRACTICE — one practice swing, looking at the target.
  • 3. SET UP — grip, stance, aim. Take your time.
  • 4. SWING — one smooth swing. Trust it. Don't overthink.
"Pick. Practice. Set up. Swing."

The Putting Grip (different from full swing)

The "reverse overlap" putting grip:

  • Both palms face each other, like clapping the club.
  • Bottom hand (right hand for righties) goes on first.
  • Top hand wraps over, and the top hand's INDEX FINGER rests over the bottom hand's fingers.
  • Hands move as ONE unit. Wrists stay still.
"Hands together. Wrists quiet."

Reading a Green

1️⃣

Squat behind ball

Crouch behind your ball, looking at the hole. See the BIG slope of the green.

"Behind the ball."
2️⃣

Look at the hole

Walk to the hole. Crouch on the OTHER side. Look back at your ball. See the slope from there too.

"From both sides."
3️⃣

Pick a line

Imagine a curved line from your ball to the hole. Pick a spot along that line to roll the ball OVER.

"Curve = the line."
4️⃣

Pick a speed

Uphill = harder. Downhill = softer. Aim to roll the ball 12-18 INCHES PAST the hole if you miss.

"Past the hole, not short."
5️⃣

Trust it & roll

Set up over the ball. ONE last look at the target. Smooth pendulum. Trust your line.

"One look. Smooth roll."

The Warm-Up Menu

Every session starts with 5-10 minutes of warm-up. Pick 3-4 moves from the menu below — different combos work for different days.

🤸 Body Wake-Up

Arm circles, hip swings, shoulder rolls, side bends. 30 seconds each. Golf is rotation — wake the body up first.

"Wake the body."

✋ Grip Practice

Hold the club. Check the grip. Both pinkies touching. Soft hands. Do it 5 times until it feels right.

"Hands together. Soft."

🦵 Stance Check

Feet shoulder-width. Soft knees. Tilt from the hips. Ball in the middle. Look in a mirror or have Daddy check.

"Tilt from hips."

🟢 Putting Pendulum

5 short putts from 3 feet. No wrists — just shoulders rocking. Build the rhythm.

"Shoulders, not hands."

⛳ Chip Brush

5 small chips. Practice BRUSHING the grass under the ball. Don't dig.

"Brush. Don't dig."

🔄 Slow Swing

5 slow full swings. NO ball. Feel the body turn. End with belly facing target.

"Smooth. Finish balanced."

🎯 Aim Drill

Lay a club on the ground pointing at a target. Set your feet PARALLEL to it. Like train tracks. Step in.

"Train tracks."

🐢 Pre-Shot Routine

Pick. Practice. Set up. Swing. 3 reps with no ball — just the routine.

"Same routine, every shot."

👀 Target Spotting

Pick a far target (a tree, a flag). Look at it. Then look at your ball. Look at target. Smooth swing.

"Eyes find target."

🤫 Quiet Body

Address the ball. Hold STILL for 3 seconds. Then swing. Builds the "stay still until impact" habit.

"Still body. Then swing."

The 4 Lesson Formats

A

FORMAT A · DISCOVERY

Phase 1 (Weeks 1–6) + sprinkled throughout

Pure foundations. The basics: hold the club, stand, putt, chip. Backyard or putting green or carpet. No course pressure. Repeat the same moves until they feel natural.

  • 5 min
    Warm UpPick 3 moves from the menu. Same ones each time at first. Routine is the goal."Same warm-up = ready body."
  • 15 min
    Skill BlockOne specific skill — putting from 3 feet, or grip check, or chip brushing. Just that."One thing. Many reps."
  • 10 min
    GameA small game using the new skill. Closest to the pin. Ladder putting. Keep it fun."This is the best part."
  • 5 min
    Exit RitualClubs back in the bag. Water. High five. "Good practice.""Wrap it up clean."
B

FORMAT B · DRIVING RANGE

Starts Week 7 — alternates with A

The range. A bucket of balls. Hit shots with structure. Each ball has a target. Each ball has a purpose. NOT just smashing balls.

  • 8 min
    Warm UpMenu moves. Plus 5 short putts on the practice green if available."Wake the body."
  • 10 min
    Short GameChip and putt practice. Closer is more important than further."Short game first."
  • 20 min
    Range ShotsA bucket. Every shot: pick target, pick club, full pre-shot routine, swing. No racing."Every ball, a routine."
  • 5 min
    Cool Down5 last putts. End on a make if you can. Pack up."End on a good one."
C

FORMAT C · ON COURSE

Starts Week 27 — alternates with A and B

Real holes. Real flags. Real score. Walk if you can — it teaches the game. Daddy is the playing partner and coach (sparingly). Etiquette is as important as the score.

  • 10 min
    Range Warm-Up10 putts. 10 chips. 5 full swings. Quick. Just to get loose."Loose, not tired."
  • 5 min
    First Tee RoutineQuiet. Pick a target. Pre-shot routine. Smooth swing. Go."Same routine as range."
  • 60-90 min
    Play the Holes3, 6, or 9 holes depending on stamina. Honest count. Repair greens. Rake bunkers."Walk briskly between shots."
  • 5 min
    Exit RitualHat off. Shake hands. "Good game." Sign the scorecard if you kept one."Hand shake. 'Good game.'"
D

FORMAT D · GOLFER

Starts Week 40 — the highest format

Ownership and reflection. You choose the focus. You pick the round goal. You lead the group's pace. And at the end, you name one win and one challenge — the metacognitive frame that real golfers build.

  • 5 min
    My GoalPick ONE focus for today. "Smooth tempo." Or "find the fairway." Write it down."One focus. One goal."
  • 10 min
    My Warm-UpYOU pick 3 menu moves. YOU pick the order. Daddy just watches."My routine."
  • 12 min
    Tactical ChallengeA round with one specific rule — "no driver today" or "putt with eyes closed on lag putts." Stretches the game."One rule. One round."
  • 5 min
    ReflectionAfter the round: 1 thing you did well, 1 thing to work on. Write or say out loud."What I learned today."

The 52-Week Plan

A full year of practice. Phase 1 (weeks 1-6) is pure Format A — putting foundations. Phase 2 (7-26) adds Format B — the chipping and full swing build at the range. Phase 3 (27-39) adds Format C — real golf on the course. Phase 4 (40-52) adds Format D — ownership and the golfer mindset.

Phase 1 · Putting Foundations

Week 1A

Discover the Clubs

Lay out the 4 main clubs (putter, wedge, iron, driver). Name each. Hold each. Notice how different they feel.

Week 2A

The Grip

How to hold the club. Both pinkies together. Soft hands. Practice until it feels natural.

Week 3A

The Stance

Feet shoulder-width. Soft knees. Tilt from the hips. Ball in the middle of your stance.

Week 4A

The Putter

The shortest club. Shoulders rock like a pendulum. NO wrists. Putt across the carpet to a cup.

Week 5A

Short Putts

3-foot putts. Goal: make 10 in a row. Build confidence before going longer.

Week 6A

Longer Putts

6-foot putts. Then 10-foot. Distance control is harder — focus on smooth tempo.

Phase 2 · Adding Chipping & Swing

Week 7A

The Wedge

New club: the wedge (for chipping). Heavy bottom. Designed to slide UNDER the ball.

Week 8B

Chip Brush Drill

Brush the grass under the ball. No big swings. Just brush.

Week 9A

Short Chip

A 5-foot chip onto the green. Ball flies a little, rolls more.

Week 10B

Chip to a Target

Set up a hula hoop or chalk circle. Chip into it. 10 attempts.

Week 11A

Longer Chip

10–15 feet now. Slightly bigger backswing. Same brushing motion.

Week 12B

Chip & Putt

Chip the ball onto a "green." Then putt it in. Real golf in miniature.

Week 13A

The 7-Iron

New club: the 7-iron. Longer than the wedge. The most useful "fairway" club for kids.

Week 14B

Small Swing With Iron

HALF backswing only. Slow. Smooth. Brush the grass. Ball goes 20 feet.

Week 15A

The Pre-Shot Routine

Pick. Practice. Set up. Swing. Same 4 steps. Every shot.

Week 16B

Routine Practice

10 chips with the pre-shot routine. Same order, same tempo, every time.

Week 17A

The Full Swing

Bigger backswing now. Turn the shoulders. Unwind through. End balanced.

Week 18B

Range Day

First trip to the driving range. 30 balls. Slow. Focus on contact, not distance.

Week 19A

Aim & Alignment

Train tracks. Clubface at target. Feet parallel. Practice with two clubs on the ground.

Week 20B

Aim Drill

Use an alignment stick. Set up. Hit. Check: did the ball go where you aimed?

Week 21A

Reading the Green

How to see slope. Crouch behind the ball. Crouch behind the hole. Pick a line.

Week 22B

Slope Putting

Put a ball on a slope. Read it. Pick a line. Putt. Did it curve as you predicted?

Week 23A

The Driver

The longest club. Hardest to hit. Use only when ready. Tee the ball HIGH.

Week 24B

Driver Day

Range. 10 driver shots. Easy swing. Distance comes from CONTACT, not effort.

Week 25A

Etiquette Review

The 10 rules. Quiet. Fore. Honors. Repair. Rake. Don't walk on lines. Honest count.

Week 26B

Practice Round (3 holes)

End-of-phase celebration. First REAL holes on a course. Pick any 3. Take your time.

Phase 3 · Adding Course Play

Week 27C

First 9-Hole Round

Format C debuts. 9 holes with Daddy. Goal: enjoy it. Don't worry about score.

Week 28A

Picking the Right Club

New skill: how far does each club go? Roughly. Use the SHORTER club if unsure.

Week 29B

Club Selection Drill

Range. Daddy calls a distance. You pick the club. Hit. Did you choose right?

Week 30C

Hole-by-Hole Strategy

Walk a 9-hole round. Before each hole: "what's my plan?" Talk it out with Daddy.

Week 31A

Bunker Shots

New skill: hitting OUT of a sand trap. Open the clubface. Swing through the sand BEHIND the ball.

Week 32B

Practice Bunker

10 sand shots at the practice bunker. Goal: get out, every time.

Week 33C

Full Round (9 holes)

9-hole round with focus on the rules. Mark a scorecard.

Week 34A

Reading the Wind

New skill: wind changes everything. INTO the wind = pick more club. WITH wind = less club.

Week 35B

Wind Drill

Range on a windy day. Hit shots in different directions. Notice the wind effect.

Week 36C

9-Hole with Scoring

Real round. Real score. No mulligans (do-overs). Count every stroke honestly.

Week 37A

Trouble Shots

Ball in the rough. Ball behind a tree. New skill: take your medicine — hit a safe shot, not a hero shot.

Week 38B

Trouble Practice

Drop a ball in tough spots. Practice getting out. Goal: back to the fairway.

Week 39C

9 Holes with a Goal

End-of-phase. Set a goal before the round (e.g. "fewer than 60 total"). Try to beat it.

Phase 4 · Becoming a Golfer

Week 40D

I Choose the Round

Format D debuts. Alexander picks the goal (par, score, focus). Daddy listens.

Week 41A

Course Management

New skill: plan TWO shots ahead, not one. Where do you want to be for your next shot?

Week 42B

Plan the Hole

Before each hole, talk through TWO shots. Then hit them.

Week 43C

9 Holes — Big Round

A "real" round. Take your time. Apply everything. No bail-outs.

Week 44D

One Focus, One Round

Pick ONE thing to focus on for the whole 9 holes. "Smooth tempo." Or "find the fairway." Just that one thing.

Week 45A

Mental Game

New idea: bad shots happen. Move on FAST. The next shot is the only one that matters.

Week 46B

Reset Drill

Daddy intentionally makes you hit a bad shot. Then practice the RESET — deep breath, walk forward, next shot is fresh.

Week 47C

9 Holes with a Friend

Invite a cousin or buddy. Real round. Real etiquette. Real fun.

Week 48D

I Lead the Group

Alexander takes charge: calls honors, watches pace, reminds everyone to repair the green.

Week 49A

Tracking Your Game

New skill: keep a simple stats card. Fairways hit. Greens hit. Putts per hole.

Week 50B

Stats Round

9 holes. Track everything. After: what's your strength? What needs work?

Week 51C

First 18-Hole Round (when ready)

The full deal. 18 holes. Pace yourself. Eat a snack. Stay hydrated.

Week 52D

Golf Birthday

Year-end. Reflect: 3 favorite rounds. Set a goal for Year 2. Celebrate.

Drills get the body strong. Games get the spirit strong. Mix them in every practice. Some work great in the backyard. Some need a course or driving range. Some are perfect when Nicolas joins.
⛳

Closest to the Pin CLASSIC

The classic golf challenge. Pick a target (a flag, a cone, a chalk circle). Each player gets ONE chip. Whoever lands CLOSEST to the target wins. Mark each ball with a tee.

Why it's perfect for the brothers: built-in turn-taking (one shot at a time), instant feedback, no winner-vs-loser drama (you're racing toward a target), and Nicolas and Alexander can use different clubs at different distances to make it fair.

"One chip. Closest wins."
🥅

Putting Contest

10 putts from the same spot. Whoever makes MORE wins. Vary the distances. Loser racks up the balls.

"Steady rhythm."
🪜

Ladder Putting

Set up 5 balls at 2 feet, 4 feet, 6 feet, 8 feet, 10 feet. Make them in order. Miss = start over.

"Build the ladder."
🎯

Chip the Hoop

Lay a hula hoop on the lawn. 10 chips. How many land IN the hoop? Move the hoop further each round.

"Land it in."
🚧

Tin Can Chip

A big plastic bucket on its side, 20 feet away. Chip balls into it. CHING! when you make it.

"Aim for the can."
🎈

Pop the Balloon

Tie balloons to stakes in the yard at different distances. Chip until you POP one. Move on.

"Pop the target."
🔢

21

Putting game. Make a 3-footer = 1 point. Make a 6-footer = 2 points. Make a 10-footer = 3 points. First to 21 wins.

"Points add up."
🏆

Up & Down

Chip onto the green. Then putt out. The combo = 2 strokes. Did you "get up and down"? Aim for it.

"Chip. Putt. Done."
🐢

Slow Swing Contest

Who can swing the SMOOTHEST and the SLOWEST and still hit the ball? Tempo over power.

"Smooth. Slow. Solid."
🎲

Club Roulette

Daddy randomly picks a club for you. You have to figure out how to hit the shot with THAT club. Builds creativity.

"Figure it out."
📏

Distance Discovery

At the range. Hit 5 shots with each club. Pace off the average distance. Write it down. Build your "yardages."

"Know your numbers."
🚲

Cart Path Only

Walking 9 holes? Try carrying or pulling. Walking golf is the BEST way to learn the game. Real feel for distance.

"Walk. Don't ride."

Make any drill more fun

  • Add a story. "You're tied for the lead at the Masters. This shot is for the green jacket."
  • Add silly cues. "Swing like you're stirring honey."
  • Add a target. A bucket, a hula hoop, a tin can. Aim is more fun than just hitting balls.
  • Add a count. "How many chips in a row land in the circle?" Counting builds focus.
  • Add a victory move. Fist pump after a great putt. Twirl the club. Make it dramatic.
  • Add music. Bring a speaker to the backyard. Swing to a beat.

The SouthCoast has lots of golf options for kids. A few worth knowing:

  • Bay Pointe Country Club (Buzzards Bay) — public-access, family-friendly, junior programs.
  • The Bay Club at Mattapoisett — beautiful course, private but worth knowing about for junior programs and tournaments.
  • The Kittansett Club (Marion) — historic, exclusive, and right in town. Limited access but a stunning seaside course.
  • Reservation Golf Club (Mattapoisett) — public, walker-friendly, gentle for beginners.
  • Acushnet River Valley Golf Course — public, scenic, good practice facilities.
  • Whaling City Golf Course (New Bedford) — public municipal, great for first rounds, junior rates.

Best starting points for kids: any course with a practice green and chipping area where you can drop a basket of balls and just putt and chip without paying for a full round.

🏌️ Junior golf programs & resources

  • The First Tee — Southeastern Massachusetts — firsttee.org. The national gold standard for youth golf. Teaches the game AND the values. Affordable. Look for the local chapter.
  • US Kids Golf — uskidsgolf.com. THE brand for junior clubs sized by height. They also run a junior tour.
  • PGA Junior League — pgajrleague.com. Team-based, scramble-format youth golf. Great social experience.
  • LPGA*USGA Girls Golf — if applicable. Free or low-cost girls-only programs.
  • PGA Coach — find a local PGA Professional who teaches juniors. The first 5 lessons are gold.

🏃 Integrating his PT with golf

Golf pairs surprisingly well with PT work. The swing requires rotation, balance, and core stability — all Sarah territory.

  • Rotational core strength. The golf swing IS a coiled rotation. Sarah's rotational core work translates directly. Ask her to add resisted trunk rotations.
  • Hip mobility. The backswing and follow-through need hip rotation. Tight hips = a flat, weak swing. Sarah's hip mobility work pays off.
  • Single-leg balance. The finish position of a full swing is essentially a single-leg balance on the lead leg. Sarah's single-leg work helps.
  • Shoulder mobility. A full backswing needs supple shoulders. Sarah can prescribe overhead mobility work.
  • Breathing during the swing. Golf is a focus sport. Pre-shot breath work (one slow exhale) lowers tension. Pair with Sarah's breathing exercises. Given the respiratory profile being investigated, golf is gentle — long walks between short bursts of effort. Very respiratory-friendly.
  • Walking endurance. A 9-hole walk is 2+ miles of variable-terrain walking. Builds endurance gently.

🏌️ Equipment for junior golf

  • Junior clubs sized by height. US Kids Golf is the standard. Get fitted at a shop or via their height chart. Wrong-size clubs make the swing impossible to learn. Replace every 1-2 years as the kid grows.
  • Starter set (5-7 clubs). Driver, 7-iron, wedge, putter is the bare minimum. Adding a hybrid and a 9-iron makes a good 5-club bag for beginners.
  • Junior bag. Light. Stand bag (legs that fold out) is best for walking.
  • Glove. One glove for the LEAD hand (left hand for righties). Improves grip. Replace yearly.
  • Tees. A pocket-full of wooden tees. Long tees for the driver, short tees for irons.
  • Balls. Cheap practice balls at first. ProV1s later (much later).
  • Hat / visor. Sun protection. Plus it looks like real golf.
  • Cleats / spikes. Soft spikes only (not metal). Or grippy sneakers — many courses allow them.
  • Ball mark repair tool. A tiny two-pronged tool. Carry in your pocket. Repairs the green after a shot lands.
  • Divot tool / repair sand. Replace divots. Fill with sand if available.

🧠 Alexander-Specific Suggestions

  • Pre-round routine. Same words, same order. "Bag. Glove. Water. Hat. Sunscreen. Tees. Go." Predictability lowers transition anxiety.
  • Walking is the secret. Don't rent a cart. Walking the course builds endurance, gives breaks between shots, and is genuinely fun.
  • Start with 3 holes, not 18. Short sessions on the course build positive associations. Long ones build fatigue.
  • Snacks on the course. A familiar safe snack between holes. Apple, crackers, water. Critical for energy and emotional regulation.
  • Visual schedule on the bag. Same as the other sports: velcro pictures.
  • Pre-load videos. Watch a junior golf drill the night before each new skill.
  • End every session on a good shot. One that felt great, or one that just barely went in. Memory anchors.
  • No score pressure early. Don't keep score for the first many rounds. Just play. Score comes later.
  • Time it with the weather. Hot days are hard. Early morning or evening rounds are gentler.
  • Practice greens are FREE. Most courses let kids putt and chip on the practice green for free or very cheap. Best ROI in golf.
Golf is not just a sport. It's a quiet practice in self-regulation, focus, patience, etiquette, and joyful effort. A skill that opens up beautiful courses around the world — and is something you can do with your family, your friends, your future kids. Every round is a developmental gift.
